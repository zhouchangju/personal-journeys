# Narrative and visualization lenses

Use these as choices, not as a mandatory formula. Start with what the material can honestly support.

| Narrative need | Useful visual form | Guardrail |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Show when things changed | timeline or annotated sequence | Keep dates and order exact; do not imply causality from proximity alone. |
| Explain why an attempt was hard | constraint map, schedule, or available-time comparison | Show the actual constraints before presenting a judgment. |
| Compare before and after | paired metrics, score bars, or before/after prose | Do not imply that one metric explains the whole person. |
| Show repeated practice | loop, attempt log, or feedback cycle | Include failed attempts and adjustment, not only the final result. |
| Establish credibility | document image, note excerpt, or evidence trail | Check privacy and caption what the evidence does and does not prove. |
| Explain a consequence | event → decision → consequence chain | Distinguish what was known at the time from what was learned later. |
| Express reflection | quiet prose, margin note, or ending section | Avoid turning a nuanced reflection into a universal lesson. |

## Hero’s Journey as an optional lens

The classic sequence can be a useful diagnostic vocabulary:

`ordinary context → trigger → threshold → attempts → setbacks → revised approach → return or changed understanding`

For a real personal journey, omit stages that are not true. A qualification exam may have no mentor, no dramatic threshold, and no satisfying “return.” The important story may instead be that a practical motivation changed because a policy changed, or that a near miss exposed forgotten fundamentals. Let the facts determine the shape.

## Other useful lenses

- **Constraint-to-adjustment:** what the person had available, what failed under those conditions, and what changed.
- **Evidence trail:** claim, supporting artifact, interpretation, and uncertainty.
- **Two timelines:** what happened in the moment versus what the person understood afterward.
- **Decision tree:** choices made, alternatives rejected, and consequences—not a decorative flowchart.
- **Practice loop:** attempt, feedback, revised method, next attempt.

Use one primary lens and at most one supporting lens per section. Too many frameworks make a personal story feel like a method textbook.

## Voice test

Prefer concrete scenes and plain transitions: “当时我以为……后来发现……” or “这件事本身不难，难的是……”. Preserve small facts that explain behavior. Remove slogans, generic “成长/蜕变” language, and conclusions that the body has not earned.
